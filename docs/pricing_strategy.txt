Pricing Strategy Document (PSD)
1. Purpose
This document defines the pricing philosophy, monetization strategy, value metrics, pricing evolution model, and long-term revenue strategy for the Self-Observing Testing System (SOTS).
The purpose of this document is to ensure that pricing remains aligned with:
* Product value
* Customer outcomes
* Platform evolution
* Infrastructure costs
* Market positioning
This document serves as the authoritative source for all future pricing, packaging, metering, and billing decisions.
2. Pricing Vision
The objective of SOTS pricing is not to charge customers for telemetry.
The objective is to charge customers for confidence in software behavior.
Customers do not purchase:
* Events
* Logs
* Traces
* Storage
Customers purchase:
* Workflow visibility
* Coverage visibility
* Behavioral understanding
* Quality confidence
* Release confidence
Pricing shall reflect perceived customer value rather than implementation details.
3. Pricing Principles
PSD-001 Value-Based Pricing
Pricing shall be aligned with customer outcomes.
Customers pay for:
* Applications analyzed
* Workflows understood
* Quality visibility gained
Customers shall not be required to understand internal platform architecture in order to understand pricing.
PSD-002 Simplicity First
Pricing must be understandable within minutes.
Customers should immediately understand:
* What they are buying
* What limits exist
* What causes additional cost
Complex billing structures shall be avoided unless required by platform economics.
PSD-003 Transparency
All pricing units shall be explicitly defined.
No hidden conversion systems shall exist.
No opaque credit systems shall exist.
Every billable unit must be understandable and measurable by customers.
PSD-004 Pricing Evolves With Product Value
Pricing models shall evolve as platform capabilities evolve.
Each major platform phase introduces new customer value and may introduce new pricing dimensions.
Pricing evolution must remain predictable and logical.
PSD-005 Cost Alignment
Pricing must eventually reflect infrastructure consumption.
However:
Infrastructure cost shall never become the primary pricing mechanism unless customer value scales directly with that cost.
4. Market Position
Traditional Observability Vendors
Examples:
* Datadog
* New Relic
* Sentry
* PostHog
These platforms primarily monetize telemetry volume.
Customers purchase observability.
Pricing is commonly based on:
* Events
* Sessions
* Logs
* Traces
* Hosts
SOTS Position
SOTS is not primarily an observability platform.
SOTS is a Behavioral Quality Intelligence Platform.
Customers purchase:
* Workflow understanding
* Missing flow detection
* Missing state detection
* Coverage analysis
* Quality visibility
The primary value is behavioral intelligence rather than telemetry collection.
5. Pricing Evolution Framework
SOTS pricing evolves through three eras.
Era 1 — Behavioral QA Platform (Phase 1)
Pricing Objective
Maximize adoption and category creation.
The goal is to make it easy for developers to experience:
"Demonstrate your application once and instantly discover missing flows, missing states, and QA coverage gaps."
Primary Value Drivers
Customers receive value from:
* Developer Demonstration Mode
* Behavioral Graph Generation
* Workflow Discovery
* Coverage Reports
* Missing Flow Reports
* Missing State Reports
* Session Replay
Primary Pricing Metric
Applications
Secondary Metrics:
* Team Members
* Storage
* Demonstration Sessions
Event volume shall not be used as a customer-facing pricing metric.
Strategic Rationale
Event volume is not strongly correlated with customer value during Phase 1.
Two customers may generate dramatically different event volumes while receiving similar quality intelligence.
Charging based on events would create unnecessary pricing complexity and weaken product positioning.
Era 2 — Production Intelligence Platform (Phase 2)
Pricing Objective
Align pricing with ongoing production monitoring value.
New Customer Value
Customers now receive:
* Production Monitoring
* Workflow Health Monitoring
* User Journey Intelligence
* Behavioral Trend Analysis
* Continuous Observation
Pricing Model
Base Subscription
* Application Limits
* Included Event Pool
* Event Overage Pricing
Strategic Rationale
In Phase 2:
* Infrastructure costs increase
* Customer value increases with observation volume
Event volume becomes a meaningful value metric.
Event-based pricing becomes appropriate because customers are receiving continuous behavioral intelligence from production traffic.
Era 3 — Autonomous Quality Intelligence Platform (Phase 3)
Pricing Objective
Monetize computational intelligence.
New Customer Value
Customers receive:
* Test Generation
* Regression Detection
* Failure Simulation
* Quality Intelligence
* AI Assistant
* Autonomous Validation
Pricing Model
Base Subscription
* Event Consumption
* AI Consumption
AI Consumption Principles
AI-assisted functionality shall be billed separately from telemetry.
Customers shall always understand:
* What was consumed
* Why it was consumed
* How much it cost
No opaque AI credit systems shall be used.
Published conversion rules are mandatory.

7. Enterprise Strategy
Enterprise pricing shall be customized based on:
* Application volume
* Deployment model
* Compliance requirements
* Support requirements
* Data retention requirements
* Self-hosting requirements
Enterprise agreements may include annual contracts and negotiated pricing.
8. Success Metrics
The pricing strategy shall be considered successful when:
1. Customers understand pricing without sales assistance.
2. Pricing reflects customer value.
3. Pricing scales with platform maturity.
4. Revenue scales with infrastructure costs.
5. Enterprise expansion remains viable.
6. Free users can experience core product value.
7. Upgrades occur naturally as customer maturity increases.
8. Pricing supports long-term platform sustainability.
9. Governance Rules
Any pricing change must:
1. Preserve pricing transparency.
2. Preserve customer trust.
3. Align with platform value.
4. Avoid hidden billing mechanisms.
5. Maintain compatibility with existing customer contracts.
6. Be documented in the Pricing & Packaging Specification.
This document is the authoritative source for pricing philosophy and pricing evolution across all phases of SOTS.
